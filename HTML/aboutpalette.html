About

what is `palette`?

My name is Mr. Lorem. I am 78 years old, and I’ve spent more than six decades behind a camera, chasing light and shadow in all their fleeting, poetic forms. While the world around me has changed in color, pace, and tone, I have remained rooted in the timeless clarity of black and white photography.

Monochrome has always spoken to me in a language color never could. It strips away distraction and reveals the soul of a scene—the raw emotion in a face, the elegance of form, the weight of silence, the power of contrast. For me, photography is not about documenting what is seen, but what is felt. A moment suspended in silver tones tells a deeper truth.

My journey began in my late teens, in the darkroom of my father’s home, where I first watched an image slowly appear in a tray of developer. I was hooked from the very first print. Over the years, I’ve traveled extensively—urban alleyways, abandoned houses, quiet forests, old train stations, bustling markets—always looking for stories carved in light.

Now, in the quieter stretch of life, I find even more beauty in the stillness. I walk slower, notice more, and frame with greater intention. My camera is an old friend—worn, trusted, and always patient. I still shoot with film whenever I can, hand-process my work, and believe there’s something sacred in waiting for a photograph to emerge.

This collection is a reflection of the moments I’ve found and the ones that found me. It’s not about perfection—it’s about presence. I hope, through these black and white frames, you see what I saw. Or better yet, what I felt.

Thank you for stopping by.

– Mr. Lorem
